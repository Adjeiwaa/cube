What We Offer

Scholarship

One of the most pressing needs in higher education today is funding for scholarships. Scholarships help deserving students attain their educational goals, meet their professional objectives and succeed to their fullest ability. It also enable practicing professionals to take advantage of opportunities for continuing professional development.

Mentorship

If you want success then it takes hard work, hard work and more hard work. But it also takes a little help along the way. If you are determined and enthusiastic then people will support you.– Richard Branson (Virgin Brand).Beneficiaries will be exposed to professional mentors to help guide them throughout their studies.

Leadership Education

For future leaders, it is important to realise that thorough natural personality is important, boosting leadership skills is often crucial to success. Leadership training programmes and courses, mentoring and building up relevant work experience through volunteering and internships would be invaluable to their professional and career development.

Industrial Placement

The core objective of our industrial placement is to help make our beneficiaries industry-ready by the time they graduate.

Volunteering

Volunteering is about working with others to make a meaningful contribution to society. People volunteer for a variety of reasons - to gain experience, meet new people, or expand their network of friends as a way to start a new career. Others want to give back to their community etc. Scholarship beneficiaries would be encouraged to undertake voluntary assignments.

Scholarship Management Service

Setting up an Educational Scholarship Fund can be a way to give back to your community or honor a loved one or in the case of an organization, to fulfill your social responsibility objectives. However, doing it yourself can be very time-consuming and expensive.

K-Cube would provide scholarship administration services for funds, individuals or corporate institutions based on their own defined criteria. Their criteria must however be objective and nondiscriminatory and must meet our core principles.
